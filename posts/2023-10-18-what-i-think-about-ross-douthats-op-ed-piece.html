It's hard to make a strong argument on the ethics of allowing children on social media. I should be more clear. It's hard to make any argument on the ethics of social media. Social media is such an unkempt beast, It's influence touches the lives of about half the population of the EARTH. Every other person alive has social media in some way, and that's scary.

It's scary for an 11 year old to have access to the collective knowledge of billions of people in their pocket. They can find anything on there, and they do. Something interesting, something sad, something lewd, something hateful, something informative, something entertaining, something malicious.

The way large social media companies have been run the last 20 years or so is increasingly more invasive and addictive. It's not good for developing minds. Or any minds really. But what's going to stop the chug of these massive cash machines? People like using them so why would they stop?

I think Douthat's prescription would alleviate some adolescent struggles but due to the way every adolescent ever is many would slip through the systems that keep them out. That begs the question: Were the systems in place intentionally made to be so easy to bypass? I sure think so. More users, more engagement, more money.

There's such a societal pressure to have social media. You have to have it. If you don't you'll be left out, you'll be uninformed. It's so easy to sign up, why don't you have it? Why is that? Was that the intention of these apps all along? Why are there so many ads about things I've only said out loud?
